About Me

~# whoami

I'm a Cybersecurity Engineer from Ecuador with deep expertise in offensive security, applied cryptography, and binary analysis. My approach is rooted in understanding systems at a fundamental level — from the mathematics behind cryptographic primitives to exploitable vulnerabilities at the binary layer.

I work as a technical trainer and instructor in cybersecurity and machine learning, designing and delivering hands-on programs that bridge theory with real-world attack simulation. I believe in learning by breaking: CTF competitions, lab environments, and offensive tooling are my primary research playground.

// research interests

Post-quantum cryptography — lattice-based constructions (CRYSTALS-Kyber, Dilithium) and their practical tradeoffs against classical attackers. Adversarial machine learning and AI model security. Memory corruption exploitation targeting modern mitigations (ASLR+PIE, stack canaries, full RELRO, NX). Cryptanalysis: RSA, ECC, AES, discrete logarithm problems.
