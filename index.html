About Me

~# whoami

I'm a Cybersecurity Engineer who enjoys dismantling software to understand its internals — focused on offensive security, reverse engineering, and binary analysis at a low level. I rely deeply on mathematics to research cryptography and quantum computing, while exploring how to integrate AI for more efficient vulnerability detection.

My approach is not only technical but scientific: I look for the logic behind every system I analyze, understanding not just how to break it, but how to strengthen it from its design. I'm driven by constant curiosity and the belief that learning never ends.

I dedicate my free time to CTF competitions, detailed writeups, and collaborating on academic research in applied mathematics, chemistry, statistics and quantum computing alongside university professors and fellow researchers. I also develop technical content and labs for universities and actively mentor newcomers to the field through CyberMinds EPN — because open, collaborative knowledge is what truly drives the community forward.
